Green Turtle

Recognized for their beautiful green-colored shells, Green turtles are herbivorous marine reptiles with a gentle nature. Young Green Turtles are mainly carnivores. They have a profound impact on seagrass beds, which serve as essential feeding grounds for various marine species. The Green Turtle is the most commonly found turtle in Sri Lanka. They are also found in the Indian, Pacific and Atlantic Oceans

Hawksbill Turtle

Admire the stunning intricacy of the Hawksbill turtle's shell patterns, which make them one of the most visually captivating creatures in the ocean. These critically endangered turtles are vital to coral reef ecosystems as they help maintain the health and diversity of coral reefs through their feeding habits.

Loggerhead Turtle

With their large heads and powerful jaws, Loggerhead turtles are known for their remarkable swimming capabilities and navigational prowess. These fascinating creatures undertake incredible journeys across vast oceanic distances, making them the true explorers of the sea. The Loggerhead Turtle is rare in Sri Lanka and is more commonly found on the East Coast of America. They are usually red and brown in colour and as its name suggests, is easily identifiable because of its large head.

Leatherback Turtle

Immerse yourself in the world of the largest turtle species on Earth, the Leatherback turtle. What sets them apart is their unique leathery skin, making them distinct from other turtle species with hard shells. In addition, they have a unique blood circulatory system for a cold-blooded reptile which means they are able to keep their blood warm even in cold waters using metabolic heat from their muscle activity. They embark on incredible migrations in search of their primary prey—jellyfish.
